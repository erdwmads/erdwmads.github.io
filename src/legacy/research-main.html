Research

Cosmic Mineralogy

My main research interest is Cosmic Mineralogy: the study of minerals and mineralogical processes in extraterrestrial materials, especially primitive Solar System materials.

Main interest

Cosmic Mineralogy

I am interested in how minerals in meteorites and asteroid-returned samples preserve information about nebular condensation, parent-body aqueous alteration, low-temperature reactions, and the early evolution of the Solar System.

Graduation Research

Dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 Chondrite

My graduation research focuses on dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 chondrite, aiming to constrain the formation conditions of primitive chondritic constituents through mineralogical, chemical, and crystallographic observations. The project connects carbonate formation, clay-mineral surfaces, aqueous alteration, and the broader Dolomite problem.

Topics

Carbonaceous chondrites

Primitive meteorites preserve mineralogical and chemical records of early Solar System environments and parent-body processes.

Ryugu and Bennu

Asteroid-returned samples provide clean reference materials for comparing primitive asteroids with meteorite collections.

Electron microscopy and microanalysis

I am interested in connecting texture, chemistry, and crystallography using SEM, EPMA, TEM, EDS, EELS, EBSD, XRD, and related analytical methods.

Analytical Pathway

From target grain to formation model

01Sample selection

Identify suitable Orgueil CI1 material and carbonate-bearing regions.

02SEM / BSE observation

Document morphology, texture, and spatial relation to clay-rich areas.

03EPMA mapping

Constrain elemental distribution and carbonate chemistry.

04Single-grain XRD

Investigate crystallographic features and carbonate ordering.

05TEM / EDS / diffraction

Resolve nanoscale interface, defects, and microstructural constraints.

06Formation condition model

Integrate observations to discuss primitive chondritic constituent formation.
