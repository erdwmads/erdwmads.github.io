プロジェクト情報

Orgueil CI1コンドライト中のドロマイトの鉱物学的研究：原始的コンドライト構成物質の形成条件の制約

This project investigates dolomite in the Orgueil CI1コンドライト at micro- to nanometre scales. The aim is to constrain the formation conditions of primitive chondritic constituents by examining dolomite morphology, chemical composition, crystallographic features, and its spatial relationship with clay-rich regions.

The planned approach combines SEM/EPMA observations, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations to connect occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features.

SOL 041 / 2026-05-11

ミッションログ005 · 乾式研磨と樹脂再含浸の計画

After discussing the preparation procedure with Associate Professor Taiga Okumura, I decided to use dry polishing because CI chondrite material should not be exposed to water. The embedded sample was polished using #3000, #4000, #6000, #8000, #10000, and #15000 lapping film sheets.

The polishing result was not satisfactory. When polishing beyond #4000, new scratches appeared on the sample surface. After reviewing previous NASA preparation documentation, I considered that the resin had probably not fully penetrated into the meteorite material. During polishing, small mineral grains could therefore be detached repeatedly, producing fine scratches on the surface.

A possible treatment is to impregnate the sample surface with resin again and then repeat the polishing process. Because this procedure would be time-consuming and complicated, I obtained new Orgueil particles from Professor Takashi Mikouchi. I plan to carry out resin embedding, polishing, and further sample preparation again at the 鉱物学研究室, 早稲田大学, together with Associate Professor Taiga Okumura.

SOL 031 / 2026-05-01

ミッションログ004 · エタノール冷却によるIsoMet切断

At 東京大学総合研究博物館, The University of Tokyo, 使用機材：d an IsoMet saw to cut the mounted resin-embedded Orgueil sample. Ethanol was used as the cooling liquid during cutting instead of water, in order to avoid direct water exposure during mechanical processing of the hydrous, fine-grained meteorite material.

After cutting, I carried out rotary-disc polishing in sequence using #1000, #3000, and 3 µm diamond paste. However, the polishing result was not fully satisfactory. I therefore planned to bring the sample to 早稲田大学 and ask Associate Professor Taiga Okumura for advice on the next preparation step.

LOG 003

SOL 027 / 2026-04-27

ミッションログ003 · 切断準備とガラス片への接着

At 東京大学総合研究博物館, The University of Tokyo, I carried out cutting preparation for the cured resin-embedded sample. The cured mount was first cut and adjusted so that the Orgueil particles embedded in Petropoxy 154 could be handled more safely during the following preparation steps.

The resin-embedded sample was then attached to a glass slide using adhesive in order to support the material during further cutting. After mounting, the sample was kept at 80 °C for one day to cure the adhesive and stabilise the sample–glass assembly.

LOG 002

SOL 020 / 2026-04-20

ミッションログ002 · Petropoxy 154によるOrgueil粒子の樹脂包埋

I received Orgueil particles from Professor Takashi Mikouchi and carried out resin embedding at 東京大学総合研究博物館, The University of Tokyo. The particles were embedded using Petropoxy 154 in preparation for subsequent cutting, mounting, and polishing.

After vacuum impregnation, the embedded sample was kept at 80 °C for one day to allow the resin to cure. This step was intended to stabilise the fragile Orgueil material before mechanical processing.

LOG 001

SOL 001 / 2026-04-01

ミッションログ001 · 卒業研究計画の確定

I began my graduation research project, “Mineralogical Study of Dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 Chondrite: Constraining the Formation Conditions of Primitive Chondritic Constituents.” The project asks how dolomite in the Orgueil CI1コンドライト records the formation conditions of primitive chondritic constituents and how its occurrence relates to clay-rich regions.

The initial plan is to investigate dolomite occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features using SEM/EPMA, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations across micro- to nanometre scales.
