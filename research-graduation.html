Project Info

Dolomite and clay minerals in Orgueil CI chondrite

This project investigates dolomite and clay minerals in the Orgueil CI chondrite at micro- to nanometre scales. The aim is to understand whether dolomite formation is related to clay-mineral surfaces and whether this relationship can provide a mineralogical perspective on the “Dolomite problem.”

The planned approach combines SEM/EPMA observations, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations to connect occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features.

Timeline
2026/04/01

Graduation research plan established

I began my graduation research project on the relationship between dolomite and clay minerals in the Orgueil CI chondrite. The central question is whether dolomite in primitive carbonaceous chondrites is spatially, chemically, and crystallographically associated with clay minerals, and whether this relationship can provide insight into the “Dolomite problem.”

The initial plan is to investigate dolomite occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features, especially near clay-rich regions. The project will connect SEM/EPMA, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations across micro- to nanometre scales.
