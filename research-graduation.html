Project Info

Mineralogical Study of Dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 Chondrite: Constraining the Formation Conditions of Primitive Chondritic Constituents

This project investigates dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 chondrite at micro- to nanometre scales. The aim is to constrain the formation conditions of primitive chondritic constituents by examining dolomite morphology, chemical composition, crystallographic features, and its spatial relationship with clay-rich regions.

The planned approach combines SEM/EPMA observations, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations to connect occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features.

LOG 006

SOL 052 / 2026-06-01

Mission Log 006 · Epocure 2 resin embedding and demoulding

On 29 May 2026, I carried out a new resin-embedding operation at the Mineralogy Laboratory, Waseda University, using Buehler Epocure 2 resin. A rubber mould was first coated with a release agent, and the CI chondrite particles were fixed to the bottom of the mould with Aron Alpha adhesive. After the mixed resin was poured into the mould, the sample was placed in a vacuum chamber for impregnation.

The processed sample was then left undisturbed in a clean room-temperature area until 1 June 2026. On 1 June, the sample was demoulded, yielding the resin-embedded material shown below. In Fig. 1, the sample on the left was prepared previously at The University Museum, The University of Tokyo, whereas the sample on the right was prepared at the Mineralogy Laboratory, Waseda University.

The next planned operation is cutting with a diamond wire saw on 10 June under the guidance of Associate Professor Taiga Okumura. Because ion milling requires liquid nitrogen for temperature control, and CI chondrite is sensitive to thermal effects, the ion-milling schedule will be arranged according to the arrival date of liquid nitrogen.

LOG 005

SOL 041 / 2026-05-11

Mission Log 005 · Dry polishing and resin re-impregnation plan

After discussing the preparation procedure with Associate Professor Taiga Okumura, I decided to use dry polishing because CI chondrite material should not be exposed to water. The embedded sample was polished using #3000, #4000, #6000, #8000, #10000, and #15000 lapping film sheets.

The polishing result was not satisfactory. When polishing beyond #4000, new scratches appeared on the sample surface. After reviewing previous NASA preparation documentation, I considered that the resin had probably not fully penetrated into the meteorite material. During polishing, small mineral grains could therefore be detached repeatedly, producing fine scratches on the surface.

A possible treatment is to impregnate the sample surface with resin again and then repeat the polishing process. Because this procedure would be time-consuming and complicated, I obtained new Orgueil particles from Professor Takashi Mikouchi. I plan to carry out resin embedding, polishing, and further sample preparation again at the Mineralogy Laboratory, Waseda University, together with Associate Professor Taiga Okumura.

SOL 031 / 2026-05-01

Mission Log 004 · Isomet cutting with ethanol cooling

At The University Museum, The University of Tokyo, I used an IsoMet saw to cut the mounted resin-embedded Orgueil sample. Ethanol was used as the cooling liquid during cutting instead of water, in order to avoid direct water exposure during mechanical processing of the hydrous, fine-grained meteorite material.

After cutting, I carried out rotary-disc polishing in sequence using #1000, #3000, and 3 µm diamond paste. However, the polishing result was not fully satisfactory. I therefore planned to bring the sample to Waseda University and ask Associate Professor Taiga Okumura for advice on the next preparation step.

LOG 003

SOL 027 / 2026-04-27

Mission Log 003 · Cutting preparation and glass-slide mounting

At The University Museum, The University of Tokyo, I carried out cutting preparation for the cured resin-embedded sample. The cured mount was first cut and adjusted so that the Orgueil particles embedded in Petropoxy 154 could be handled more safely during the following preparation steps.

The resin-embedded sample was then attached to a glass slide using adhesive in order to support the material during further cutting. After mounting, the sample was kept at 80 °C for one day to cure the adhesive and stabilise the sample–glass assembly.

SOL 020 / 2026-04-20

Mission Log 002 · Orgueil particles embedded in Petropoxy 154

I received Orgueil particles from Professor Takashi Mikouchi and carried out resin embedding at The University Museum, The University of Tokyo. The particles were embedded using Petropoxy 154 in preparation for subsequent cutting, mounting, and polishing.

After vacuum impregnation, the embedded sample was kept at 80 °C for one day to allow the resin to cure. This step was intended to stabilise the fragile Orgueil material before mechanical processing.

LOG 001

SOL 001 / 2026-04-01

Mission Log 001 · Graduation research plan established

I began my graduation research project, “Mineralogical Study of Dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 Chondrite: Constraining the Formation Conditions of Primitive Chondritic Constituents.” The project asks how dolomite in the Orgueil CI1 chondrite records the formation conditions of primitive chondritic constituents and how its occurrence relates to clay-rich regions.

The initial plan is to investigate dolomite occurrence, morphology, chemical composition, and crystallographic features using SEM/EPMA, single-grain XRD, and TEM observations across micro- to nanometre scales.
