Research

Research interests

I am interested in the mineralogical and chemical information preserved in primitive Solar System materials, especially carbonaceous chondrites and asteroid-returned samples.

Carbonaceous chondrites

Primitive meteorites preserve records of nebular processes, parent-body aqueous alteration, and the early chemical evolution of the Solar System.

Ryugu and Bennu

Asteroid-returned samples provide exceptionally clean records of primitive materials and allow detailed comparisons with CI/CM-like meteorites.

Mineral microanalysis

I am interested in applying electron microscopy and microanalysis to fine-grained extraterrestrial materials.

Possible directions

Hydrous minerals in primitive materials

Hydrous phases, especially phyllosilicates, can record the timing, temperature, and chemistry of parent-body aqueous alteration.

Sulfides and alteration textures

Sulfide–phyllosilicate associations may provide constraints on fluid activity, redox conditions, and low-temperature mineral reactions in asteroid parent bodies.

Analytical methods

I am interested in SEM, EPMA, TEM, EDS, EELS, EBSD, XRD, and related methods for connecting microtextures with chemical and crystallographic information.
