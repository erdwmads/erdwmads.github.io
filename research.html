Research

Cosmic Mineralogy

My main research interest is Cosmic Mineralogy: the study of minerals and mineralogical processes in extraterrestrial materials, especially primitive Solar System materials.

Main interest

Cosmic Mineralogy

I am interested in how minerals in meteorites and asteroid-returned samples preserve information about nebular condensation, parent-body aqueous alteration, low-temperature reactions, and the early evolution of the Solar System.

Topics

Carbonaceous chondrites

Primitive meteorites preserve mineralogical and chemical records of early Solar System environments and parent-body processes.

Ryugu and Bennu

Asteroid-returned samples provide clean reference materials for comparing primitive asteroids with meteorite collections.

Electron microscopy and microanalysis

I am interested in connecting texture, chemistry, and crystallography using SEM, EPMA, TEM, EDS, EELS, EBSD, XRD, and related analytical methods.
